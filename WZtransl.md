CHAPTER 1  
 
On the state of mixture of the Good Spirit and the Bad Spirit  
 
(1) It is thus revealed in the Religion that the light (was) above and the darkness below, and 
between the two there was an open space. (2) Ohrmazd (was) in the light, Ahreman in the 
darkness, and Ohrmazd knew of Ahreman's existence and also that he would come to fight 
him, (but) Ahreman did not know of the existence of the light and Ohrmazd. (3) Ahreman 
lived in the darkness, in the lower regions, so he ascended, by chance, to the upper regions, 
and he saw a ray of light; and because of the difference in n ature from his own, he strove to 
attain it, and to have power over it in the same way as over the darkness</p></text></page><page><number>15</number><text><p>(4) When he reached the boundary, Ohrmazd, with a view to driving Ahreman from his own 
kingdom, presented himself to battle, and with a pure and divine word, he confounded him 
and cast him back into darkness. To protect himself from the Druz, he created in  the mēnóg 
state in the highest (place), the mēnōg of the sky, water, earth, plants, cattle, man, and fire, 
and he preserved (them) for three thousand years.  
 
(5) Ahreman  also prepared means in the darkness. At the end of the three thousand years, 
he returned to the boundary and threatened, saying: "I will strike you, I will strike your 
creatures, the creatures you created in the mênőg state, tol who are the Good Spirit, I  will 
destroy everything with this."  
 
(6) Ohrmazd replied: "No, O Druz, you are not all -powerful !" 
 
(7) Again Ahreman threatened (him): "I will cause every bony being to hate you and love 
me."  
 
(8) Ohrmazd in (his) Menogian wisdom saw that Ahreman was capable of doing the very 
thing he had threatened (to accomplish), if the time of the fight were not limited.  
  
(9) Accordingly, he called upon Time for help, for he saw that Ahreman would not agree with 
the judgment of any luminary. Time is for both (parties)  and is necessary for cooperation and 
a fair settlement,

(13) And by the Ahunawar he was shown three kinds (of things).  
 
(14) First, that everything good is the will of Ohrmazd  
 
(15) From this it is clear that if good is the will of Ohrmazd, it is clear that there is something 
that is not according to the will of Ohrmazd, inevitably this (comes) from a principle of a 
different substance.  
 
(16) Secondly, (there is) this: he who does the will of Ohrmazd will possess reward and 
retribution, (but) he who does not do the will of Ohrmazd will possess chastisement and 
punishment.  
 
(17) By this is manifested the reward of the virtuous and the punishment of sinners, and the 
reason for the existence of paradise and hell, by which the aptitude for opposition is 
explained.  
 
(18) Thirdly, it was shown that he who [gives protection] to the poor increases the kingdom 
of Ohrmazd  
 
(19) He showed that the rich must help the needy, just as educated people teach the 
ignorant, and those who are rich in goods give generously to those in need, that is, the help 
of the creatures of Ohrmazd to one another in an energetic struggle. (20) Thus  the 
accomplishment of Renovation can be done by these three  
 
things:  
 
(21) First, the right religion which (is) itself a profession of two principles, in this way and 
manner that Ohrmazd (is) all goodness and devoid of malice, and his will is all good, (while) 
Ahreman (is) all malice  and devoid of goodness .</p></text></page><page><number>17</number><text><p>(22) Second, the hope of reward and retribution in the virtuous, the fear of chastisement and
